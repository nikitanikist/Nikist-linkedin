--- 1.3 DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPTS ---

DISCOVERY CALL OPENING (First 2 minutes):
---
"Hi [Name], I was really looking forward to this call. Thanks for taking the time.

So here's how this works: this call is for ME to understand:
1. Your business and background
2. What you're trying to achieve on LinkedIn
3. What specific results you're looking for

Then I can show you if/how we can help.

Sound good? So can you start by telling me - what does your business do,
and what's brought you to thinking about [their problem/need]?"

---

DISCOVERY CALL KEY QUESTIONS (10-Question Framework):
---
1. "Can you walk me through your business model?"
2. "What's been your biggest challenge with [their pain point]?"
3. "How long have you been dealing with this?"
4. "What have you tried so far?"
5. "What's it costing you (time/money) that you're not solving this?"
6. "What would success look like for you?"
7. "What kind of results would make this worth your while?"
8. "What's your timeline? When would you want to see results?"
9. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
10. "If we could solve this, what would that mean for your business?"

---

DISCOVERY CALL STRUCTURE:
---
PHASE 1 - RAPPORT (2 minutes):
- "Hi [Name], thanks for the time"
- Light personal connection
- State purpose clearly

PHASE 2 - PROBLEM DISCOVERY (8-10 minutes):
- Ask about their business
- Deep dive into their specific pain
- Listen more than you talk
- Take notes on their exact language

PHASE 3 - SOLUTION POSITIONING (3-5 minutes):
- Share relevant case study
- Position your solution to their problem
- Don't pitch yet - just contextualize

PHASE 4 - QUALIFICATION (3-5 minutes):
- Ask budget/timeline questions
- Understand decision-making structure
- Gauge seriousness

PHASE 5 - NEXT STEPS (2 minutes):
- "Based on this conversation, here's what I'm thinking..."
- Propose solution option
- Schedule follow-up/proposal discussion
- Get email for proposal

---

DISCOVERY CALL - BUDGET CONVERSATION (If they ask "How much does this cost?"):
---
DON'T answer immediately.

Instead: "Great question. Before I quote, let me understand better.

You mentioned [their goal]. How much would it be worth to you
if we delivered that result in [timeline]?"

Listen to their number.

Then: "Perfect. Here's what we typically do:

We have a few options:
• Consulting at $[lower price]/month - where I guide your team
• Done-with-you at $[mid price] - where we work together
• Done-for-you at $[higher price] - where we handle everything

Where would make most sense given your situation?"

--- 1.4 SALES CONVERSATION - OBJECTION HANDLING ---

OBJECTION A: "I need to think about it"
RESPONSE:
"Totally understand.

Just so you know, we have limited capacity this month/quarter.

But no pressure - if you decide to move forward, just let me know.

In the meantime, here's [resource] to help you think through the decision."

---

OBJECTION B: "It's too expensive"
RESPONSE:
"I hear you. Budget is always a consideration.

Here's the thing though: what's the cost of NOT doing this?

You mentioned [their pain]. How much is that costing you annually in lost [revenue/time/opportunity]?

Usually our clients see ROI in [timeframe]. So it pays for itself."

ALTERNATIVE:
"If budget is tight, we can customize:
• Just start with [smaller scope]
• Or consulting model where I guide your team
• Then scale up as you see results"

---

OBJECTION C: "We're already working with someone"
RESPONSE:
"Got it. How's that going? Are you seeing the results you want?

[Listen]

The reason I ask is we've found most of our clients come to us because their previous
solution wasn't delivering [specific outcome].

If you're ever open to exploring a different approach, I'm here."

---

OBJECTION D: "I want to see case studies/proof"
RESPONSE:
"Great question - I always want clients who ask for proof.

Here's what we can do:
• [Option 1] Small project ($X) where we prove our approach
• [Option 2] I share a case study for similar client
• [Option 3] Free audit/analysis so you can see our work

Which appeals to you?"

---

OBJECTION E: "Can you send me something in writing first?"
RESPONSE:
"Sure, happy to.

But before I put together a proposal, quick question:
if we could deliver [their stated goal], would this be worth pursuing?

[Listen]

Perfect. Let me put together something custom. Expect it by [day]."

---

OBJECTION F: "I don't have time to discuss this"
RESPONSE:
"No problem - I respect that.

15 minutes is all I need to show you if this could work.

How about [specific time day after tomorrow]?

Or I can just send over a quick [proposal/case study] if that's easier."

SECTION 3: PERSONALIZATION STRATEGIES - THE SECRET SAUCE

--- 3.1 FIRST-LINE PERSONALIZATION (CRITICAL) ---

THE RULE: The first line of your outreach message MUST be fully personalized.
Everything else can be template, but first line = 100% custom.

WHY: Busy founders/prospects auto-delete non-personalized openers.
If first line doesn't show you know them specifically, they don't read further.

FIRST-LINE EXAMPLES:

BASED ON RECENT POST:
"Loved your post about [specific topic they posted] - the part about [specific insight]"

BASED ON COMPANY/ROLE:
"Saw you're now at [company] as [role] - congrats on that move"

BASED ON PROFILE RESEARCH:
"Noticed you've been [specific action visible on profile] - that shows you're thinking about [topic]"

BASED ON MUTUAL CONNECTION:
"[Mutual connection] mentioned you're exploring [specific thing]"

BASED ON PUBLISHED CONTENT:
"Read your article on [topic] - the framework about [specific point] is gold"

BASED ON TIMING SIGNAL:
"Noticed your post about [hiring/fundraising/expansion] - what a great time for [specific observation]"

THE TEMPLATE + VARIABLE MODEL:
---
TEMPLATE (80% of message):
"I've been helping [similar companies] achieve [specific outcome].

We typically help with [specific problem] and deliver [specific result].

Should I include [analysis/framework] or should we jump on a call?"

VARIABLE (20% - The First Line):
Fully custom based on their profile, post, or company

RESULT: Looks completely personalized, scalable to 100s of people
---

--- 3.2 PERSONALIZATION-AT-SCALE FRAMEWORK ---

THE INSIGHT: You can't personally hand-craft 500 messages.
But you can use ChatGPT + automation to make each one feel personalized.

PROCESS:

STEP 1: Create Template Message
Create one core message that works. Example:
"Hi [NAME],

I noticed you're working on [SPECIFIC_DETAIL from their profile].

I help [SIMILAR_PEOPLE] achieve [OUTCOME].

We've seen [SPECIFIC_RESULT] for companies like [SIMILAR_COMPANY].

Should I include [LEAD_MAGNET] or should we jump on a call?"

STEP 2: Identify Variables for Each Prospect
For each person in your outreach list:
- [NAME] = Their first name
- [SPECIFIC_DETAIL] = Their recent post, company, role change, etc.
- [SIMILAR_PEOPLE] = ICP that matches them
- [OUTCOME] = What they need (from your research)
- [SIMILAR_COMPANY] = A case study company in similar industry
- [LEAD_MAGNET] = Your lead magnet

STEP 3: Use ChatGPT to Generate Variations
Prompt for ChatGPT:
"Create 10 variations of this first-line opener for outreach messages:
[Template with blanks]

For these prospect profiles:
1. [Prospect 1 details]
2. [Prospect 2 details]
...

Make each opener feel unique and personalized based on their LinkedIn profile."

STEP 4: Combine Custom Opener + Template Body
Custom First-Line (from ChatGPT) + Template Rest of Message

STEP 5: Automation
Use Heritage/Instantly/Make to send automatically with:
- Custom first line per person
- Same template body
- Personalized variable fills (name, company, etc.)

RESULT:
Each prospect gets a message that feels 100% custom, but you only wrote the template once.

--- 3.4 PERSONALIZATION PSYCHOLOGY ---

WHY PERSONALIZATION WORKS:
• Founders/busy decision-makers get 50+ generic messages per week
• They auto-delete anything that doesn't show you know them
• Personal touch (even if automated) makes them feel special
• Personalization = effort perception = higher response rate

THE TWIST: Most people know it's at-scale personalization
But if the VALUE is real and CUSTOMIZATION is genuine (their specific metrics, their specific problem),
they engage anyway.

PROOF FRAMEWORK:
Even if personalization is templated + automated, if:
✓ Value is real and useful
✓ Customization is specific to their profile/company/problem
✓ Effort appears high (beautiful presentation)
✓ Outcome is actionable

...they'll engage despite knowing it was sent to 100 others

--- 4.2 TIMING & SEQUENCE ---

TIMING CONSIDERATION:
✓ Monday - Thursday best for outreach (better response rates)
✗ Friday-Sunday lower response rates
✓ 9 AM - 12 PM best sending window
✓ Afternoon (2-5 PM) secondary window

DECEMBER/HOLIDAY EFFECT:
• Lower reply rates expected (vacation, holiday planning)
• BUT: Companies showing "intense signals" still looking for help
• Strategy: Target companies with clear buying signals / active post engagement

SEQUENCE:
Step 1: Send connection request (NO message in request - higher acceptance)
Step 2: Wait 2-5 days for acceptance
Step 3: Once accepted, review their profile carefully
Step 4: Send DM with personalized first-line + your message

BEST PRACTICE: Send connection without message in request itself

WHY:
• Higher acceptance rate (less "spammy" feeling)
• Message acceptance: 70-80% without request note
• Message acceptance: 40-50% with request note mentioning something

ONCE ACCEPTED:
Then you can message with full personalization

This gives you time to:
• Research them properly
• Customize your first-line opener
• Find specific detail to reference
• Make message feel truly personalized

--- 5.1 MULTI-STEP FOLLOW-UP FRAMEWORK ---

THE PRINCIPLE: Don't take initial silence as "no"

SILENCE INTERPRETATION:
• Day 1-2: Too early to judge
• Day 3-5: They might be busy
• Day 7+: Worth sending follow-up
• Day 14+: Last attempt worth trying
• Day 30+: Can write-off unless specific reason

FOLLOW-UP TIMING WINDOWS:

After Initial Message:
Day 0 (same day): Send message
Day 3: Light follow-up if no response
Day 5-7: Value-add follow-up
Day 10: Last soft attempt
Day 14: Final outreach attempt

After Discovery Call:
Day 0 (same call day): Send call summary
Hour 3-4 (within 3-4 hours): Send proposal/next steps
Day 3: Check in on proposal
Day 5: Answer any questions
Day 7: Closing attempt
Day 14: Final follow-up if no decision

After Proposal Sent:
Day 0: Send proposal
Day 2-3: "Did you get it? Any questions?"
Day 5: "Thoughts on the proposal?"
Day 7: "Where do we stand?"
Day 10: "Still interested or should I send alternatives?"
Day 14: "Last check - moving forward or passing?"

Add: Free resource or different angle

Only if they showed any sign of interest OR

--- 6.3 BUDGETING CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK ---

THE PRINCIPLE: Don't quote until you understand value to them

STEP 1 - Understand Value:
"You mentioned you want to achieve [their goal].
How much revenue/time/opportunity would that be worth if we delivered it?"

STEP 2 - Listen to their number:
They tell you what it's worth to them.
(Usually 3-5x what you're going to charge)

STEP 3 - Position Price in Context:
"Perfect. So if we delivered [goal] worth $[their number] in [timeframe],
would an investment of $[your price] make sense?"

STEP 4 - Offer Tiers:
"We typically have a few options:
• Option A at $[price] - [what you do]
• Option B at $[higher price] - [what you do]
• Option C at $[lower price] - [what you do]

Where makes sense?"

STEP 5 - Justify with Process, Not Price:
Don't explain WHY you charge X.
Instead, explain WHAT they get + RESULTS they'll see.

Your process = the justification

---

--- 6.4 CLOSING TECHNIQUES ---

ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE:
"So let's get started. I'll send over the contract tonight.
We can kick off on [date]."
(Assumes yes, makes it easy to agree)

ALTERNATIVE CLOSE:
"So does [option 1] or [option 2] work better for you?"
(They choose between two yeses)

DEADLINE CLOSE:
"We have limited capacity this month.
If you want to start now, I can fit you in.
After that, next availability is [date]."

TRIAL CLOSE:
"Want to do a small project first to see if we're a good fit?
$[smaller price] and we deliver [result]."

SECTION 9: WARM vs COLD OUTREACH - DIFFERENT STRATEGIES
================================================================================

--- 9.1 WARM OUTREACH (Profile Viewers, Referrals, Warm Knock Points) ---

DEFINITION: "Warm knock point" = outreach to someone already aware of your solution

EXAMPLES OF WARM CONTACTS:
• Profile viewers (visited your LinkedIn)
• Warm referrals (mutual connection introduced)
• Past prospects (showed interest but timing was wrong)
• Community members (in same LinkedIn group/course)
• Email subscribers (downloaded your lead magnet)

WARM OUTREACH MESSAGING:
✓ Reference their awareness of you
✓ Acknowledge their interest signal
✓ Make it easy to continue conversation

TEMPLATE:
"Hi [Name],

Saw you [specific warm signal].

Since you're exploring [solution], thought I'd reach out.

[Lead magnet/value offer] might be helpful.

Interested?"

CONVERSION RATES:
• Response rate: 20-30% (high)
• Meeting conversion: 30-40%
• Close rate: 20-30%

KEY CHANNELS:
• Profile viewers (highest priority)
• Warm referrals (second highest)
• People you've engaged with on posts
• LinkedIn group members
• Past website visitors

---

--- 9.2 COLD OUTREACH (Strangers, No Prior Contact) ---

DEFINITION: Outreach to someone with zero prior relationship to you

EXAMPLES:
• Sales Navigator list (no prior interaction)
• Email list (cold email)
• Prospecting new companies
• Ad-generated leads who don't know you yet

COLD OUTREACH CHALLENGES:
✗ Low response rate (1-3%)
✗ Requires more personalization to stand out
✗ Takes longer to build trust
✗ Higher rejection/being ignored

COLD OUTREACH BEST PRACTICES:
✓ Maximum personalization (reference specific detail about them)
✓ Use trust signals (case studies, social proof)
✓ Lead with value (lead magnet, not pitch)
✓ Expect longer sales cycle

TEMPLATE:
"Hi [Name],

[SPECIFIC PERSONALIZATION - they know you've researched them]

I help [similar people] achieve [specific outcome].

[PROOF/CASE STUDY]

Should I [value offer] or should we [call offer]?"

CONVERSION RATES (Cold):
• Response rate: 1-3% (low)
• Meeting conversion: 10-20%
• Close rate: 5-10%

COLD OUTREACH VOLUME:
Must do 600-1000+ outreach/month to get consistent results
(Due to lower response rate)

---

--- 9.3 WARM vs COLD - STRATEGY MIX ---

OPTIMAL APPROACH: 70% Warm + 30% Cold

WHY:
• Warm outreach has 10x better conversion
• But limited supply of warm contacts
• Cold outreach needed for scale
• Mix = predictable revenue + growth

WARM PRIORITY:
1. Profile viewers (first priority)
2. Warm referrals (second priority)
3. Past prospects (third priority)
4. Community members (fourth priority)

COLD STRATEGY:
• Once you've exhausted warm contacts
• Use Heritage/Instantly for scale
• Target highly specific niches
• Include strong lead magnets
• Accept lower conversion rates

SECTION 10: ANTI-PATTERNS - WHAT NOT TO DO
================================================================================

--- 10.1 COPY-PASTE FAILURES ---

MISTAKE: Send generic message to everyone
RESULT: Auto-delete from busy prospects
FIX: First line must be personalized (see Section 3.1)

MISTAKE: Copy entire message from template
RESULT: Looks spammy, low response
FIX: Customize first line, feel free to use template body

MISTAKE: Generic openers like "Hi there" or "Hope you're doing well"
RESULT: Instant deletion
FIX: Reference something specific about them in first line

---

--- 10.2 BAD TIMING & SEQUENCING ---

MISTAKE: Send discovery call proposal 2 weeks after conversation
RESULT: They forget context, don't engage
FIX: Send within 3-4 hours of call (see Section 1.3)

MISTAKE: Never follow up after initial message
RESULT: Miss 70% of opportunities (people need multiple touches)
FIX: Follow-up sequence at Days 3, 5, 7, 14 (see Section 5)

MISTAKE: Follow up too aggressively (daily)
RESULT: Look desperate, get blocked
FIX: Space follow-ups 3-5 days apart

MISTAKE: Outreach without profile optimization
RESULT: People visit profile, not convinced, leave
FIX: Optimize profile FIRST (see Section 9.1)

---

--- 10.3 MESSAGE MISTAKES ---

MISTAKE: "Hi, I offer XYZ services. Interested?"
RESULT: Straight deletion
FIX: Offer value first, ask second (see Section 1.1)

MISTAKE: Message is all about you/your service
RESULT: No engagement
FIX: 70% about THEM, 30% about you

MISTAKE: No clear call-to-action
RESULT: They don't know what to do next
FIX: Always end with clear next step (see templates)

MISTAKE: Trying to close in first message
RESULT: Looks like sales pitch, deleted
FIX: First message = start conversation, not close deal

MISTAKE: Talking about price in first message
RESULT: Objections/disqualification
FIX: First message = value, discovery call = price

---

--- 10.4 STRATEGY MISTAKES ---

MISTAKE: Outreach before optimizing profile
RESULT: High conversion rate wasted (bad profile kills it)
FIX: Week 1 = Profile optimization, Week 2+ = Outreach

MISTAKE: Outreach without content visibility
RESULT: When people visit profile, no proof of expertise
FIX: Post 30 days of content BEFORE heavy outreach (see Section 9)

MISTAKE: Blasting same message to 500 people
RESULT: Marked as spam, blocked
FIX: Use Heritage for safe automation (see Section 7.1)

MISTAKE: Only doing cold outreach, ignoring warm leads
RESULT: Low conversion rate unnecessarily
FIX: Prioritize profile viewers and referrals (see Section 9.1)

MISTAKE: Giving up after 2-3 outreach with no replies
RESULT: Not reaching critical mass for results
FIX: Consistency of 25-30/week for 3-6 months (see Section 2.1)

---

--- 10.5 POSITIONING MISTAKES ---

MISTAKE: Claiming to help everyone (no niche)
RESULT: Message doesn't resonate with anyone
FIX: Pick ONE primary ICP, be specific (see Section 4)

MISTAKE: Bio says different thing than headline
RESULT: Algorithm confused, visibility tanked
FIX: Profile must tell same story everywhere (see Section 6.2)

MISTAKE: Messaging positions you as freelancer not founder
RESULT: Can't scale, won't attract quality clients
FIX: Position as founder building systems (see Section 4.1)

   Profile viewers, referrals, warm contacts = 10x conversion rate.
   Cold outreach = need volume. Warm outreach = need quality.

7. SYSTEMS OVER HUSTLE
   Build systems (Heritage automation, follow-up sequences, lead magnets).
   Don't grind doing 500 manual messages.
   Grind building systems once, then let them run.

8. COMPOUND EFFECTS WORK

   Week 12+: Exponential growth
