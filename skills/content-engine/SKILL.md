# Content Engine — LinkedIn Growth Engine

Create LinkedIn content that generates leads, not just likes. Every post should move the needle toward meetings and revenue.

## Prerequisites

Ensure the user has a clear ICP (from ICP Builder) and optimized profile (from Profile Optimizer). Content without positioning is noise.

## Core Content Philosophy

From the course: "Your content is your runway. It warms up cold audiences so outreach converts."

The content hierarchy:
1. **Lead Magnets** > Everything else (60-70 meetings per lead magnet post)
2. **Problem-based posts** > Achievement posts > Generic advice
3. **Consistency** > Virality (compound effects beat one-hit wonders)
4. **Visibility** > Vanity metrics (100 right eyeballs > 10,000 wrong ones)

## Content Framework: Hook → Context → Insight → CTA

Every post follows this structure:

### 1. HOOK (first 2 lines — this is everything)
The hook determines if anyone reads the rest. Use these formulas:

- **Transformation Hook**: "I went from [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]"
- **Curiosity Gap**: "Most [ICP] make this mistake with [topic]. Here's what works instead:"
- **Vulnerability**: "I almost gave up on [thing]. Here's what changed:"
- **Contrarian**: "Unpopular opinion: [common belief] is dead. Here's why:"
- **Data Hook**: "[Specific number] — that's how many [impressive stat]"
- **Incomplete Sentence**: "The #1 reason [ICP] fail at [thing] is..."
- **Bold Claim**: "You don't need [common thing]. You need [your thing]."

"Broken hooks" = hooks that don't create enough curiosity to make someone click "see more." Test your hook by asking: "Would I stop scrolling for this?"

### 2. CONTEXT (next 3-5 lines)
Set up the problem or story. Keep it relatable to ICP.

### 3. INSIGHT (the meat — 5-10 lines)
Deliver value. Frameworks, steps, lessons, data. Be specific — generic advice doesn't convert.

### 4. CTA (last 1-2 lines)
Always end with a clear next step:
- "Comment [keyword] and I'll send you [resource]" (lead magnet distribution)
- "DM me [word] for [specific offer]"
- "Save this for later. Follow for more [topic]."
- "What's your experience with [topic]? Drop it below 👇"

## Post Types (in priority order)

### 1. Lead Magnet Posts (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
These outperform everything 3-4x. From the course: "One lead magnet post can generate 60-70 meetings."

Structure:
- Hook about the problem the lead magnet solves
- 3-5 bullet points of what's inside
- Visual preview (screenshot/mockup of the resource)
- CTA: "Comment [keyword] to get this free"
- Auto-send via DM or comment reply

Lead magnet types: frameworks, templates, checklists, audit tools, case studies, swipe files.

### 2. Problem-Based Posts
Talk about ICP's problems. Show you understand their world.

### 3. Results/Case Study Posts
Share client results with specific numbers. Before → After format.

### 4. Building-in-Public Posts
Document your journey. Share wins, losses, learnings in real-time.

### 5. Framework Posts
Share a proprietary framework (numbered steps or matrix).

### 6. Contrarian/Opinion Posts
Challenge industry norms. Creates engagement through debate.

## Content Calendar: 30-Day Plan

**Posting frequency**: Start with 3-4 posts/week. Scale to daily once you find your rhythm.

**Weekly template**:
- Monday: Problem-based post (pain point)
- Tuesday: Framework or how-to
- Wednesday: Lead magnet or resource
- Thursday: Case study or results
- Friday: Personal story or building-in-public

**Content batching**: Create 4-5 post templates, then rotate and customize. Use the Excel sheet system from the course — one row per post idea with columns for: Hook, Context, Insight, CTA, Post Type, Target ICP.

## Content Creation with AI

Use this workflow:
1. Generate 10 post ideas using the ICP's pain points
2. Write hooks for each using the hook formulas above
3. Draft full posts using Hook → Context → Insight → CTA
4. Review and add personal voice/examples
5. Create visual elements if needed (infographics, carousels)

**AI Tools**: Google Gemini (less hallucination), Claude for playbooks, Figma/Canva for visuals.

**Critical Rule**: Do NOT use AI-generated images in posts. LinkedIn audiences react negatively. Use real photos, screenshots, or designed graphics.

## Posting via Chrome

When the user approves a post:
1. Navigate to LinkedIn via Claude in Chrome
2. Click "Start a post"
3. Paste the content
4. Add any images/documents
5. Post immediately or schedule

## Metrics to Track

- **Impressions**: Track weekly growth (aim for 10-15K per infographic)
- **Engagement rate**: Comments matter more than likes
- **Profile views**: Should increase with consistent posting
- **Lead magnet responses**: Track comments and DMs per lead magnet post
- **Meetings booked**: The ultimate content metric

## Anti-Patterns

- Don't post without a CTA — every post should drive action
- Don't chase virality — consistency compounds
- Don't use AI images — they hurt credibility
- Don't post about topics outside your niche — confuses the algorithm
- Don't copy competitors' posts — find your unique voice
- Don't write long posts without a strong hook — nobody reads past line 2
- Don't post lifestyle content unless tied to business lessons
