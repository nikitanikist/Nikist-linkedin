# ICP Builder — LinkedIn Growth Engine

Build a comprehensive Ideal Customer Profile by asking smart questions, then create a targeting strategy that drives real LinkedIn leads.

## Core Philosophy

Never let the user skip ICP work. Every other LinkedIn activity (content, outreach, engagement) fails without a clear ICP. As the course teaches: "If you don't know who you're targeting, you're just posting into the void."

## Step 1: Discovery Interview

Ask the user these questions using AskUserQuestion (group into 2-4 at a time, not all at once):

**Round 1 — Business Foundation:**
- What do you sell? (product, service, SaaS, consulting — be specific)
- What problem does it solve? (the pain point, not the feature)
- What's your price range? (this determines which ICP tiers to target)

**Round 2 — Customer Clarity:**
- Who has already bought from you? (existing customers = best ICP signal)
- What job titles do your buyers hold? (CEO, VP Marketing, Head of Growth, etc.)
- What company size are they? (1-10, 11-50, 51-200, 201-1000, 1000+)
- What industry or vertical? (SaaS, e-commerce, agencies, manufacturing, etc.)

**Round 3 — Pain & Trigger Mapping:**
- What triggers someone to need your solution? (new funding, hiring, scaling, revenue plateau)
- What are they currently doing instead? (competitor, manual process, nothing)
- Where do they hang out on LinkedIn? (groups, hashtags, influencers they follow)

**Round 4 — Positioning Check:**
- How do you want to be perceived? (expert, partner, vendor, thought leader)
- What's your unique angle that competitors don't have?

## Step 2: Apply the 3-Layer ICP Framework

From the answers, build a structured ICP using this framework from the course:

**Layer 1 — WHO you help:**
- Job titles (primary + secondary)
- Company size range
- Industry/vertical
- Geography (if relevant)

**Layer 2 — WHAT they struggle with:**
- Top 3 pain points
- Current alternatives they use
- The cost of NOT solving this problem

**Layer 3 — WHO decides (and WHO influences):**
- Decision maker title
- Budget holder
- Champions/influencers in the org
- Buying committee structure

## Step 3: Create the Positioning Statement

Use this template:
"I help [SPECIFIC ICP from Layer 1] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT they want] using [YOUR METHOD/PRODUCT]"

This becomes the foundation for headline, bio, content, and outreach messaging.

## Step 4: Build Targeting Lists

Guide the user to find prospects using these methods (in priority order):

1. **Sales Navigator Filters** — Title + Company Size + Industry + Geography + Posted in last 30 days
2. **Profile Viewers** — People already checking you out = warm leads
3. **Competitor Followers** — Follow competitors' company pages, engage with their followers
4. **Event Attendees** — LinkedIn events in your niche
5. **Post Engagers** — People commenting on relevant content in your space
6. **Y Combinator Directory** — For SaaS targeting funded startups
7. **Crunchbase/Bombora** — For intent-based targeting (who's actively buying)
8. **Job Postings** — Companies hiring for roles your product replaces = buying signal

Target: Build a list of 600+ qualified prospects to start outreach.

## Step 5: Validate & Iterate

After the first round of outreach (50-100 messages):
- Track reply rates by ICP segment
- Identify which segment responds best
- Double down on winners, prune losers
- Refine positioning based on actual conversations

## Output Format

Deliver the ICP as a structured document with:
1. ICP Summary (one paragraph)
2. Positioning Statement
3. Target Persona Card (title, company, pain, trigger)
4. Sales Navigator Search Filters (exact filters to use)
5. Prospect Source Priority List
6. First 30-Day Targeting Plan

## Anti-Patterns — What NOT to Do

- Never target "everyone" — one clear ICP beats three vague ones
- Don't target individual contributors unless they influence buying
- Don't skip company size — a startup CEO and enterprise CEO are different ICPs
- Don't pick an ICP based on who's easy to find — pick based on who pays
- Don't change ICP every week — give it 30 days minimum before pivoting
